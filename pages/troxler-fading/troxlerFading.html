Focus on the Center

Keep your eyes fixed on the white dot in the center — do not look away or move your eyes at all.
~ The effect will not work otherwise. ~

What Just Happened?

What you experienced is called Troxler Fading. When you fixate your eyes on a central point, your brain gradually fades out the unchanging stimuli in your peripheral vision. This happens because of neural adaptation — your visual neurons stop responding to constant input to help you focus on motion or changes, which are usually more important for survival.

The interesting thing? The dots never fully disappeared. Although it may seem they did, we only reduced the brightness by about 75%.
