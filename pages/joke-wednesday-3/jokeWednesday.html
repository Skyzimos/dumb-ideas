Saying Goodbye As The Year Ends, With Memories We’ll Hold Forever

The year comes to a close as this year’s “Bravo!” performance ends on a good note. (Pun fully intended.)

On Thursday, May 22nd, Coon Rapids High School celebrated a stunning performance during their annual concert, "Bravo!" Witnesses recount audience members yelling “Thank you for the Music!” while others were seen wiping away tears after the Senior Song.

During the song “Dancing Queen,” an unidentified Soprano hit a note so high that one audience member whispered, “Is that legal?” (It is. We checked.)

Tenor Jesse, from Concert Choir, was heard singing the incorrect part for half of "Mamma Mia," even after practicing it so much he could sing it in his sleep. Oh well, maybe next time. (But let’s be honest — probably not.)

The whole night was full of unforgettable moments; a full run-through of “Waterloo” with no issues for the first time in all 18 rehearsals, a microphone being handed off last-second during “The Winner Takes It All” (accidental, but it looked intentional), a sudden—wait… is this the right script?

Behind all of the magic the night brought was a bittersweet truth; this marked the final performance for our graduating seniors.

Choir director Mr. Kainz was overheard backstage saying,

“I’m not crying, you’re crying!
It’s probably just stage fog in my eye.”

Since the concert, things have slowly returned back to normal. Well, as normal as they can get. (Let's be honest, are things ever "back to normal"?)

All we can say is "Mamma Mia! That performance was amazing!," and "You all are Super Troupers for making it through!"

Until next year, #ThankYouForTheMusic #YouAllRock
